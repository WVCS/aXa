What Is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

Facts about Mental Health

“One in five young people (age 13-18) has or will develop a mental illness in their lifetime”

“Most Americans lack access to adequate mental health treatment. 56% of American adults with mental illness did not receive care in the last year.”

“Half of all mental disorders begin by age 14 and three-quarters by age 24.”

“Depression and anxiety disorders cost the global economy $1 trillion in lost productivity each year”

    “Annual prevalence of serious thoughts of suicide, by U.S. demographic group:
  • 4.3% of all adults
  • 11.0% of young adults aged 18-25
  • 17.2% of high school students
  • 47.7% of lesbian, gay, and bisexual high school students”

“High school students with significant symptoms of depression are more than twice as likely to drop out compared to their peers”

“75% of young people with a mental health problem are not receiving treatment”

“People with severe mental illness die between 10 and 20 years earlier than the general population - an equivalent or greater impact on life expectancy than heavy smoking (which, on average, reduces life expectancy by eight to 10 years).”

“For every person affected by mental illness, £8 is spent on research – 22 times less than cancer and 14 times less than dementia”

When I was in 8th grade, I began to struggle with suicidal thoughts and depression after losing my best friend to suicide. After that I had really bad depression and I attempted to take my own life. Shortly after, my cousin commited suicide. I still struggle with anxiety and depression to this day with occasional suicidal thoughts. I sometimes leave people on read because I don’t know how to respond and I am afraid of saying the wrong thing. Mental health wasn’t really talked about in the past but my mom has been trying to be more supportive ever since she found out how much depression really was affecting me. Even though she doesn’t always know how to respond, she at least tries to listen to my problems. Westview has a very encouraging and open campus that allows people to open up to others. The biggest thing that has helped with coping is talking to other people. I began to go to a therapist after going through a rough time. Friends and family are my biggest supporters. “The darkest nights make the brightest stars”. This quote has stuck with me for most of my life. No matter what you’re going through, even if you can’t see any light, you just gotta keep going. Because once you’re out of the darkness, you realize how beautiful the light truly can be.
